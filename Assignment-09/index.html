What is needed?

  • cardboard cylinder from a roll of wrapping paper
    • 17" x 1" dia.
  • Card stock in sky blue, two shades of pink, medium blue, yellow, purple, and white
  • paper trimmer
  • hot glue gun
  • thick jute rope
  • scizzors

Wall hanging Instructable

Step 1 - start by trimming all 264 pieces into 1 1/2" x 5 1/2" strips. Broken down by colour, it goes: 1. Sky blue: 129 2. Hot pink: 30 3. Light pink: 24 4. Medium blue: 21 5. Yellow: 17 6. Purple: 7 7. White: 36

Looking something like the following.

Step 2 - Plug in the glue gun, and follow the patterning as you see it below to make your wall hanging. There are 29 rows and 9 columns. Use the hot glue gun to adhere the strips together. You just need a little bit of glue along the edge. Also, if you're using a dual temperature glue gun, make sure it's on a low temperature setting. I made my rainbow from right to left as I was figuring out the pattern, but you don't have to!

Step 3 - slide the top ends thru the cardboard cylinder of an old or nearly finished wrapping paper roll.

Last, insert some thick jute twine into the cylinder and tie a double knot. Hang your masterpiece!
